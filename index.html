“Heal Derm” :Avoiding Skin Infections Caused By Diabetes


Heal Derm

What is Heal Derm

A smartphone app in Sri Lanka aims to diagnose and treat skin infections caused by diabetes using machine learning algorithms and computer vision. The app can detect foot ulcers, nail defects, Acanthosis nigricans, and cellulitis.

Heal Derm

Background

A mobile application in Sri Lanka aims to help identify and treat skin infections caused by diabetes, such as foot ulcers, acanthosis nigricans, cellulitis and nail issues. The app will provide information on diabetes forms, enhancing patient quality of life and reducing diabetes-related deaths.

Heal Derm

Research Gap

The proposed research proposes a patient-centered approach to diabetes treatment, aiming to improve patient outcomes and reduce healthcare costs, offering a promising solution for global health.

Heal Derm

Research Problems

Diabetic foot ulcers are a chronic condition affecting millions globally and locally. Most people are unaware of the condition, and there is no easy diagnostic method at home. Even if diagnosed, they may not know what to do next. Doctors and dermatologists struggle to diagnose foot ulcers due to lack of knowledge and reliance on western medical solutions, particularly among older individuals.

Acanthosis nigricans is a skin disease caused by diabetes in Sri Lanka that is often overlooked due to lack of resources and knowledge. Despite most people experiencing neck discoloration, they are unaware of the condition. Despite the diagnosis, there is no clear understanding of the appropriate treatment for this skin disease, highlighting the need for improved healthcare systems in Sri Lanka.

Diabetes in Sri Lanka often leads to undetected skin infections like cellulitis, despite the existing system for detecting foot ulcers using image processing, as there is no other system for detecting cellulitis infections. Can image processing be used to accurately detect and diagnose cellulitis caused by diabetes based on visual characteristics of the infected area?

Nail infections are common among diabetic patients in Sri Lanka. Nail infections caused by diabetes properly Undiagnosed, it cannot be treated and cured. Despite the existing method of detecting foot ulcers by imaging, due to diabetes Due to the lack of a suitable method to distinguish between diabetic and non-diabetic nail infections.

Research Objectives

Detection of severity level of the foot ulcer caused by diabetic and non diabetic by using image processing and providing the prorper solutions by applying machine learning

Detection of Acanthosis Nigricans infections caused by diabetic and non diabetic by using image processing and providing the prorper solutions by applying machine learning

Detection of cellulitis infections caused by diabetic and non diabetic by using image processing and providing the prorper solutions by applying machine learning

Detection of nail infections caused by diabetic and non diabetic by using image processing and providing the prorper solutions by applying machine learning
